More Information About What I Do

I work in the field of Uncertainty Quantification, specifically on inverse problems (usually with models involving physics).

I am concerned about how experimental data impacts predictions.

Sometimes that means getting the most out of what limited data is available.

Other times it involves designing the experiment so that each measurement is as informative as possible.

Not all data are equally useful. I help decide what is worth measuring.

The applications of my academic research are numerous.

Examples include groundwater contamination and remediation, storm surge prediction, oil recovery or exploration, and imaging technology.

I have my advisor–Dr. Troy Butler–to thank for opening the door to such broadly applicable and practical research.
